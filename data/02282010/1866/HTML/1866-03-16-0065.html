Friday, 16th March, 1866.

Numb. 24.

Supply,—Order for Committee read; Motion made, and Question proposed, "That Mr. Deputy Speaker do now leave the Chair:"—Amendment proposed, to leave out from the word "That" to the end of the Question, in order to add the words "an humble Address be presented to Her Majesty, praying that She will be graciously pleased to appoint a Royal Commission, consisting of Military and Civilian Members, to inquire into the present constitution, system of education, and discipline of the Royal Military Academy at Woolwich, and of the Royal Military College at Sandhurst, as well as into the general treatment of the gentlemen Cadets, as also into the rules and regulations under which candidates are admitted into those Colleges,"—(Lord Eustace Cecil,)— —instead thereof:—Question put, "That the words proposed to be left out stand part of the Question:"—The House divided; Ayes 152, Noes 132.
