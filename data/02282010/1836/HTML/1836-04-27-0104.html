Mercurii, 27° die Aprilis, 1836.

Agricultural Distress,—Motion made, and Question proposed,—"That the Orders of the Day be now read:—Amendment proposed, to leave out from the word "That" to the end of the Question, in order to add the words "in the application of any surplus of the Revenue towards the reduction of the burdens of the Country, either by a remission of Taxes, or otherwise, due regard should be had to the necessity of affording proportionate relief to the Agricultural Interest:"—(Marquis of Chandos :)—Question put, "That the words proposed to be left out stand part of the Question:"—The House divided; Ayes 208; Noes 172.
