Martis, 5° die Julii, 1836.

Church of Ireland Bill,—Motion made, and Question proposed,—"That the Order of the Day for the Committee on the Church of Ireland Bill be now read:"—Amendment proposed, to leave out from the word "That" to the end of the Question, in order to add the words "an humble Address be presented to His Majesty, praying that His Majesty may be graciously pleased to direct that His subjects who are employed in Civil and Military Services abroad, may be relieved from assisting in religious ceremonies to which their own tenets are opposed,"—(Mr. Plumptre,)—instead thereof:—Question put, "That the words proposed to be left out stand part of the Question:"—The House divided; Ayes 44, Noes 38.
