Jovis, 30° die Junii, 1836.

Head-Money,—Motion made, and Question proposed,—"That any payment, or promise, or agreement to pay, any sums of money as head-money to electors at an Election of Members to serve in Parliament, whether made by a Candidate, or by any one acting on his behalf, is a gross violation of the freedom of Election, of the Order of this House, and of the rights and privileges of the Commons of the United Kingdom:"—(Mr. Hume :)—Whereupon, Motion made, and Question put,—"That the House do now adjourn:"—The House divided; Ayes 9, Noes 35.
