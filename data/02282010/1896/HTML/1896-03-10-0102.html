Numb. 39.

National Museums (London) (Opening on Sundays),—
Petitions in favour of opening Museums on Sundays;—From Aldermen and Common Councillors of the City of London;—Members of the London County Council;—and, Inhabitants of the County of London, in Public Meeting assembled; to lie upon the Table:—
Motion made, and Question proposed, "That, in the opinion of this House, it is desirable that the National Museums and Art Galleries in London should be open for a limited number of hours on Sundays, after 2 p.m., upon condition that no officer shall be required to attend on more than six days per week, and that any who may have conscientious objections shall be exempt from Sunday duty:"—(Mr. Massey-Mainwaring:)—
Amendment proposed, to leave out from the word "open," to the end of the Question, in order to add the words "from 10 a.m. to 10 p.m. on at least three week-day evenings in each week, so far as the safety of the collections will permit; but that the opening of Museums and Art Galleries on Sunday is neither necessary nor expedient, and is contrary to the ascertained wishes of those classes chiefly interested:"—(Sir Mark Stewart:)—
Question put, "That the words proposed to be left out stand part of the Question:" —The House divided; Ayes 178, Noes 93.
