Jovis, 5° die Martii, 1846.

Numb. 8.

Andover Union,—Motion made, and Question proposed, "That a Select Committee be appointed, to inquire into the administration of the Poor Laws in the Andover Union, and into the management of the Union Workhouse:"—(Mr. Etwall:) — Amendment proposed to leave out from the word "That" to the end of the Question, in order to add the words "there be laid before this House, ??? Copy of Mr. Parker's Report, and of the Evidence taken by him with reference to the crushing o??? bones in the Andover Union Workhouse, together with a Copy of all Evidence taken on the inquiry into the conduct of Mr. M'Dougall, and a Copy of all Correspondence between the Poor Law Commissioners and the Board of Guardians of the Andover Union or their Officers, from the 1st day o??? September 1845 to the 1st day of March 1846," instead thereof:—(Sir James Graham :)—Question proposed, "That the words proposed to be left out stand part of the Question:"—Amendment by leave, withdrawn :—
Another Amendment proposed, at the end of the Question, to add the words, "and into the conduct of the Poor Law Commissioners, and their late Assistant Commissioner Mr. Parker, i??? reference to the two investigations held at Andover, and into all the circumstances under which the Poor Law Commissioners called upon Mr. Parker to resign his Assistant Commissionership:"—(Mr. Christie:)—Question put, "That those words be there added :"—The House divided ; Ayes 92, Noes 69.
