Martis, 16° die Junii, 1846.

Numb. 63.

Objection taken to the vote of Mr. Patrick Stewart, who voted with the Noes, on the ground that he is a Director and Shareholder in the Caledonian Railway Company, and has a direct pecuniary interest in the rejection of the Glasgow, Dumfries, and Carlisle Railway Bill :—
Whereupon Mr. Patrick Stewart stated, that the sole direct interest that he had in the Caledonian Railway was being the holder of twenty shares, to qualify him to be a Director in that undertaking; and that he voted against the Glasgow, Dumfries, and Carlisle Railway, conceiving it to be in direct competition with the Caledonian Railway, as decided by the Legislature in the last Session.
Motion made, and Question put,—"That Mr. Patrick Stewart being a Director and Shareholder in the Caledonian Railway, and having a direct pecuniary interest in opposition to the Glasgow,
