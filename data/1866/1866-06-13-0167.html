Wednesday, 13th June, 1866.

Numb. 59.

Tests Abolition (Oxford) Bill,—considered in Committee:—
(In the Committee.)
Clause 1 :—Amendment proposed, in page 1, line 13, to leave out the words " or as a condition of exercising or enjoying any of the privileges and rights which may heretofore have been, or may hereafter be, exercised by graduates thereof:"— (Sir William Heathcote:) — Question put, "That the words proposed to be left out stand part of the Clause:"—The Committee divided ; Ayes 245, Noes 172.
