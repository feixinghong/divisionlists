B. M.

Wednesday, 4th March, 1896.

Numb. 27.

Working Men's Dwellings Bill,—Order for Second Reading read;
Motion made, and Question proposed, "That the Bill be now read a second time:"—
Amendment proposed, to leave out from the word "That," to the end of the Question, in order to add the words "in any measure for facilitating the acquisition of dwellings for the working classes by the use of public monies, the freehold should be vested in public bodies, and not in the individual,"—(Sir Charles Dilke,)—instead thereof:—
Question put, "That the words proposed to be left out stand part of the Question:"—The House divided; Ayes 276, Noes 91.
