Thursday, 23rd July, 1896.

Numb 345.

Land-Law (Ireland) Bill,—considered in Committee:—
(In the Committee.)
New Clause (Amendment as to improvements,)—(Mr. Gerald Balfour,)—brought up, and read the first and second time:—
Amendments made.
Another Amendment proposed, in line 25, after the word "custody," to insert the words,—
"For the purpose of an application to fix a fair rent, the word 'improvement' shall, notwithstanding anything in The Landlord and Tenant (Ireland) Act, 1870, be construed to mean any increased letting value due to or arising out of any expenditure of labour or capital on or in respect of the holding:"—(Mr. Kilbride:)—
Question put, "That those words be there inserted:"—The Committee divided; Ayes 121, Noes 180.
