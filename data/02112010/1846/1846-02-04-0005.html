Mercurii, 4° die Februarii, 1846.

Numb. 2.

Irish Great Western Railway,—Motion made, and Question proposed, "That it be an Instruction to the Committee on the Petition for the Irish Great Western Railway Bill, to entertain the Petitions of the Midland Great Western Railway Company of Ireland and James Malley:—Daniel Desmond and James Malley;—John Gaynor;—and, James Malley, notwithstanding the same were not presented three clear days before the day appointed for the first meeting of the Committee:"—Amendment proposed, at the end of the Question to add the words, "provided it appear to the said Committee that the said Petitions were in the possession of some Parliamentary Agent on Friday morning, and that he was prepared to place them in the hands of a Member on the same day for presentation to the House, if the House had not been adjourned over that day:"—Question put, "That those words be there added:"—The House divided; Ayes 6, Noes 39.
