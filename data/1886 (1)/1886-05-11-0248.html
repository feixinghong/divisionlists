Tuesday, 11th May, 1886.

Numb. 93.

Death Penalty,—Motion made, and Question proposed, "That, in the opinion of this House, the time has arrived for the abolition of the death penalty for the crime of murder:"—(Sir Joseph Pease:)—
Amendment proposed, to leave out from the word "House" to the end of the Question, in order to add the words "it is desirable that offences, for which the penalty of death now follows a verdict of guilty, unless remitted by Her Majesty, should be divided into three categories, as recommended bv a Royal Commission in 1866; and in order that executions, when neressary, may be carried out with every regards to humanity and decency, an experienced person should be selected by the Government for the purpose, and adeqately remunerated from public funds,"—(Mr. Howard Vincent,) —instead thereof:—
Question, "That the words proposed to be left out stand part of the Question," put, and agreed to.
Main Question put:—The House divided; Ayes 62, Noes 117.
