Friday, 12th March, 1886.

Numb. 29.

Supply,—Order for Committee read;
Motion made, and Question proposed, "That Mr. Speaker do now leave the Chair:"—
Amendment proposed, to leave out from the word "That" to the end of the Question, in order to add the words "Notices of Questions be given by Members in writing to the Clerk at the Table without reading them viva voce in the House, unless the consent of the Speaker to any particular Question has been previously obtained,"—(Sir Henry Selwin-Ibbetson,)—instead thereof:—
Question proposed, "That the words proposed to be left out stand part of the Question:"—Debate arising;
Motion made, and Question put, "That the Debate be now adjourned:"—(Mr. T. P. O'Connor:) — The House divided; Ayes 54, Noes 235.
