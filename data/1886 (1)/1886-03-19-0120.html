Friday, 19th March, 1886.

Numb. 38.

Supply,—Order for Committee read;
Motion made, and Question proposed, "That Mr. Speaker do now leave the Chair:"— Amendment proposed, to leave out from the word "That" to the end of the Question, in order to add the words "in the opinion of this House, it is not just or expedient to embark in war, contract engagements involving grave responsibilities for the Nation, and add territories to the Empire without the knowledge and consent of Parliament"—(Mr. Richard,)—instead thereof:—
Question put, "That the words proposed to be left out stand part of the Question:"— The House divided; Ayes 108, Noes 112.
