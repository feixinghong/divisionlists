A. M.

Monday, 22nd February, 1886.

Numb. 9.

East India, Burma (Expenses of Military Operations),—Motion made, and Question proposed, "That, Her Majesty having directed a Military expedition of Her forces charged upon Indian revenues to be despatched against the King of Ava, this House consents that the revenues of India shall be applied to defray the expenses of the Military operations which may be carried on beyond the external frontiers of Her Majesty's Indian possessions:"—(Sir Ugthred Kay-Shuttleworth:)—
Amendment proposed, to leave out from the word "That" to the end of the Question, in order to add the words "this House is of opinion that it would be unjust to defray the expenses of the Military operations in the Kingdom of Ava out of the revenues of India,"—(Mr. Hunter,)—instead thereof:—
Question put, "That the words proposed to be left out stand part of the Question;"— The House divided; Ayes 297, Noes 82.
