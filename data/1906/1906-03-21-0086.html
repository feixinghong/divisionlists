Wednesday, 21st March, 1906.

Numb. 23.

Evening Sitting.
South Africa (High Commissioner),—Motion made, and Question proposed, "That this House expresses its disapproval of the conduet of Lord Mil???er, as High Commissioner of South Africa and Governor of the Transvaal, in authorising the flogging of Chinese labourers in breach of the Law, in violation of treaty obligation, and without the knowledge or sanction of His Majesty's Secretary of State for the Colonies:"—(Mr. Byles:)—
Amendment proposed, to leave out from the word "House," to the end of the Question, and insert the words "while recording its condemnation of the Hogging of Chineses coolies in breach of the Law, desires, in the interests of peace and conciliation in South Africa, to refrain from passing censure upon individuals,"—(Mr. Churchill,)—instead thereof:—
Question proposed, "That the words proposed to be left out stand part of the Question:"— Debate arising;
Mr. Chamberlain rose in his place, and claimed to move, "That the Question be now put :"—
Question, "That the Question be now put,"put, and agreed to.
Question, "That the words proposed to be left out stand part of the Question," put accordingly, and negatived. Question put, "That those worlds be there inserted;"—The House divided; Ayes 355, Noes 135.
