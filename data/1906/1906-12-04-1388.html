Numb. 457.

Workmen's Compensation Bill,—As amended (by the Standing Committee), further consideration:—
Another Amendment proposed to the Bill, in page 3, line 12, after the word "cause," to insert the words "and
(b) the failure to make a claim within the period above specified shall not be a bar to the maintenance of such proceedings if it is found that the failure was occasioned by mistake or other reasonable cause:"—(Mr. Secretary Gladstone:)—
Question proposed, "That those words be there inserted in the Bill:"—
Proposed Amendment amended, by inserting, after the word "mistake," the words "employment abroad:"—(Mr. Ashley:)
Another Amendment proposed to the Amendment, as amended, at the end, to add the words,—
"Provided that the employer is not, in the opinion of the court, prejudiced in his defence:"— (Sir Edward Carson:)—
Question put, "That those words be there added to the proposed Amendment, as amended:"— The House divided; Ayes 81, Noes 296.
