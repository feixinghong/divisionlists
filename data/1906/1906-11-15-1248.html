Thursday, 15th November, 1906.

Numb. 411

Merchant Shipping Acts Amendment (No. 2) Bill,—Order read, for resuming Adjourned Debate on Amendment proposed [23rd October] on Consideration of the Bill, as amended (by the Standing Committee):—
Another Amendment proposed to the Bill, in page 29, to leave out lines 28, 29, and 30, and insert the words,—
"Lascar seamen, whilst serving on board British ships in latitudes beyond thirty-five degrees north and thirty-five degrees south, shall be provided with the same amount of space in forecastles approriated for their use as provided for Europeans, but when serving within tropical climates, that is, within the above-named latitudes, seventy-two cubic feet shall be deemed sufficient space for each Lascar seaman,"—(Mr. Havelock Wilson,)—instead thereof:—
Question put, "That the words proposed to be left out stand part of the Bill:"—The House divided; Ayes 248, Noes 105.
