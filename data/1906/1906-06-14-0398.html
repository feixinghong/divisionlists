B. M.

Thursday, 14th June, 1906.

Numb. 122.

Cork City Railways and Works Bill (by Order),—Order for Consideration, as amended, read; Motion made, and Question proposed, "That the Bill, as amended, be now considered:"—
Amendment proposed, to leave out from the word "That," to the end of the Question, in order to add the words "this House, in view of the fact that by The Fishguard and Rosslare Railways and Harbours Act, 1898, the Great Western Company and the Great Southern and Western Company undertook the obligation to construct similar works to those proposed by this Bill, declines to proceed with the Bill, on the ground that these Companies are seeking by it to evade compliance with their said undertaking to Parliament and are also in default as regards the further obligation to construct a line from Fermoy to Dunkettle which they accepted under the said Act of 189???,"—(Mr. O'Shee,)—instead thereof:—
Question put, "That the words proposed to be left out stand part of the Question:"—The House divided; Ayes 196, Noes 29.
