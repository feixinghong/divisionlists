B. M.

Tuesday, 29th February, 1876.

Numb. 13.

County Palatine of Lancaster (Clerk of the Peace) Bill,—considered in Committee :—
(In the Committee.)
Clause 1 :—Amendment proposed, in page 1, line 17, after the word " him," to insert the words " Provided always, that each deputy clerk to be appointed shall be a solicitor of ten years' standing, and shall have an office within the district for which he is appointed and the clerk of the peace shall have no interest in the emolument of any deputy clerk :"—(Mr. Rathbone :)—
Question proposed. " That those words be there inserted :"—
Amendment amended, by leaving out the word " ten," and inserting the word " seven." Another Amendment proposed to proposed Amendment, as amended, to leave out from the the word " standing," to the end of the Amendment :—(Mr. Hardcastle :)—
Question put. " That the words proposed to be left out stand part of the proposed Amendment. as amended :"—The Committee divided ; Ayes 45, Noes 53.
