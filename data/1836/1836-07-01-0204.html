Veneris, 1° die Julii, 1836.

Church of Ireland Bill,—Motion made, and Question proposed,—" That the Order of the Day for the Committee on the Bill be now read :"—Amendment proposed, to leave out from the word " That " to the end of the Question, in order to add the words " it is expedient that Tithes and all Compositions for Tithes in Ireland should cease, and be for ever extinguished ; compensation being first made for all vested interests, whether lay or ecclcsiastical ; and that it is also expedient that measures should be adopted to render the Revenue of the Church Lands more productive, and more available for the support of the working Clergy of the Establishment ; and that all persons not in communion with the Established Church Ireland should be relieved from all assessment for its support,"—(Mr. Sharman Crawford :)—instead thereof:—Question put, " That the words proposed to be left out stand part of the Question :"— The House divided; Ayes 61, Noes 18.
