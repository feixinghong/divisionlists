Veneris, 8° die Julii, 1836.

Established Church Bill,—Order for Committee read:—Motion made, and Question proposed,—"That Mr. Speaker do now leave the Chair:"—Amendment proposed, to leave out from the word "That" to the end of the Question, in order to add the words "an humble Address be presented to His Majesty, praying that He will be graciously pleased to issue His Commands to the Commissioners appointed to examine into the state of the Established Church in England and Wales, to take into consideration, and report their opinion on the expediency of abolishing the existing system of the translation of Bishops from one See to another," instead thereof:—(Mr. Charles Lushington:)—Question put, "That the words proposed to be left out stand part of the Question:"—The House divided; Ayes 124, Noes 44.
